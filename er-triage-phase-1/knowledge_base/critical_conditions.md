# Critical Condition Recognition — ER Triage Flags
Source: AHA/ACC Guidelines, Surviving Sepsis Campaign, AHA Stroke Council

## STEMI — ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction
Trigger keywords: chest pain, chest tightness, chest pressure, jaw pain, left arm pain, diaphoresis, crushing chest.
Clinical criteria: Chest pain/discomfort + any of: radiation to jaw/left arm, diaphoresis, nausea with chest pain, duration > 20 minutes, nitrates not relieving pain.
Vitals of concern: HR > 100 with chest pain, SBP < 90, new bradycardia.
Action: Activate STEMI protocol immediately. 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes. Aspirin 325mg stat. Cardiology alert. Target door-to-balloon < 90 minutes.
ESI: 1–2. Do not wait for triage completion.

## Sepsis / Septic Shock
Trigger keywords: fever, infection, confusion, weakness, decreased urine output, fast breathing, chills, rigors.
SIRS Criteria (2 or more = SIRS): Temperature > 38.3°C or < 36°C, HR > 90, RR > 20, WBC > 12,000 or < 4,000.
Sepsis = SIRS + suspected infection source.
Septic Shock = Sepsis + SBP < 90 despite 30mL/kg fluid bolus OR lactate > 4 mmol/L.
Vital flags: HR > 100 + Temp > 38.5°C + RR > 22 = high suspicion.
Action: Sepsis bundle within 1 hour: blood cultures × 2, broad-spectrum antibiotics, 30mL/kg IV crystalloid, lactate, CBC, BMP.
ESI: 2 (sepsis), 1 (septic shock).

## Acute Ischemic Stroke
Trigger keywords: facial droop, arm weakness, slurred speech, sudden headache, vision loss, confusion, dizziness with fall.
FAST Screen: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call.
BE-FAST: Balance loss, Eyes (vision change), Face, Arm, Speech, Time.
Critical window: Alteplase eligible within 4.5 hours of symptom onset. Thrombectomy within 24 hours for large vessel occlusion.
Vital flags: BP > 185/110 may contraindicate tPA. New-onset hypertension + neurological symptoms = stroke until proven otherwise.
Action: Stroke code immediately. CT head without contrast stat. Neurology alert. Last known well time documented.
ESI: 1–2.

## Pulmonary Embolism (PE)
Trigger keywords: sudden shortness of breath, chest pain worse with breathing, leg swelling, recent surgery, immobility, calf pain, hemoptysis.
Wells Score risk factors: DVT symptoms, PE most likely diagnosis, HR > 100, immobilization/surgery in past 4 weeks, prior DVT/PE, hemoptysis, malignancy.
Vital flags: SpO₂ < 92% + tachycardia (HR > 100) + pleuritic chest pain = high suspicion.
Massive PE: Hemodynamically unstable (SBP < 90), pulseless. ESI 1.
Action: CT pulmonary angiography (CTPA) stat. IV access. Heparin ready. Consider thrombolytics for massive PE.
ESI: 2 (submassive/high probability), 1 (massive PE with hemodynamic compromise).

## Hypertensive Crisis
Trigger keywords: severe headache, vision changes, chest pain, shortness of breath, confusion, epistaxis with very high BP.
Hypertensive Urgency: SBP ≥ 180 and/or DBP ≥ 120 without acute end-organ damage.
Hypertensive Emergency: SBP ≥ 180/120 WITH end-organ damage — encephalopathy, acute MI, aortic dissection, pulmonary edema, eclampsia, stroke.
Vital threshold: BP ≥ 220/140 = treat regardless of symptoms.
Action for Emergency: IV antihypertensives (labetalol, nicardipine). Reduce MAP by no more than 20–25% in first hour. ICU admission.
ESI: 2 (emergency), 3 (urgency without symptoms).

## Diabetic Emergencies
DKA Trigger: Type 1 DM + nausea/vomiting + abdominal pain + fruity breath + polyuria + polydipsia.
HHS Trigger: Type 2 DM + extreme hyperglycemia (>600) + altered mental status + severe dehydration, NO significant ketosis.
Hypoglycemia: Blood glucose < 70 + diaphoresis + tremor + confusion + palpitations. Severe: < 50 or altered mental status.
Vital flags: Tachycardia + hypotension in DKA = severe volume depletion.
Action: Fingerstick glucose stat for all altered mental status. For hypoglycemia: dextrose IV or glucagon IM immediately.
ESI: DKA = 2, Hypoglycemia with AMS = 1–2.

## Anaphylaxis
Trigger keywords: allergic reaction, bee sting, food allergy, medication reaction, hives + breathing difficulty, throat tightening, throat swelling.
Criteria: Rapid onset + skin/mucosal symptoms + any of: airway compromise, hypotension, end-organ dysfunction.
Vital flags: BP drop > 30% from baseline, HR > 100, SpO₂ < 94%, stridor.
Action: Epinephrine 0.3–0.5mg IM (anterolateral thigh) IMMEDIATELY. Supine position. IV access. Diphenhydramine + corticosteroid as adjuncts only. Monitor 4–6 hours minimum.
ESI: 1–2. Never delay epinephrine.
