# Emergency Severity Index (ESI) — Triage Criteria
Source: ESI Implementation Handbook, 5th Edition (AHRQ)

## ESI Level 1 — Immediate / Resuscitation
Patient requires immediate life-saving intervention. Any delay in treatment is life-threatening.
Indicators: unresponsive, apneic, pulseless, severe respiratory distress, active hemorrhage uncontrolled, GCS ≤ 8, refractory hypotension (SBP < 80 despite fluids), anaphylaxis with airway compromise.
Action: Physician at bedside immediately. Resuscitation room.

## ESI Level 2 — Emergent
High-risk situation or patient is confused, lethargic, or disoriented, OR patient is in severe pain or distress.
Indicators: altered mental status, signs of stroke (facial droop, arm weakness, speech difficulty — FAST positive), chest pain with diaphoresis, active seizure, SpO₂ < 90% on room air, BP ≥ 180/120 with symptoms, HR > 150 or < 40, severe abdominal pain, GCS 9–13, suspected STEMI, sepsis criteria (2+ SIRS), suicidal ideation with plan.
Action: Should not wait. Immediate room assignment. Physician notified within 5 minutes.

## ESI Level 3 — Urgent
Stable vital signs. Requires two or more resources (labs, IV, imaging, medications, procedures).
Indicators: moderate pain (4–7/10), vomiting and dehydration, mild-moderate shortness of breath (SpO₂ 91–94%), laceration requiring sutures, urinary symptoms with fever, moderate trauma.
Action: Patient may wait. Re-triage if condition changes. Target evaluation within 30 minutes.

## ESI Level 4 — Less Urgent
Requires one resource. Stable. Single complaint with straightforward workup.
Indicators: mild pain (1–3/10), minor lacerations, sprains, rashes without systemic symptoms, UTI without fever, medication refill.
Action: Target evaluation within 60 minutes.

## ESI Level 5 — Non-Urgent
No resources required. Could be managed in primary care.
Indicators: chronic complaint, routine prescription request, minor cold symptoms, known chronic condition with no acute change.
Action: Consider redirection to urgent care. Target evaluation within 120 minutes.

## ESI Vital Sign Thresholds for Downgrade to ESI 2
Any of the following should trigger reassessment for ESI 2 upgrade:
- SpO₂ < 90%
- HR < 50 or > 150
- SBP < 80 or > 200
- RR < 8 or > 36
- Temperature > 41°C or < 35°C
- GCS < 14
- New-onset confusion or agitation
