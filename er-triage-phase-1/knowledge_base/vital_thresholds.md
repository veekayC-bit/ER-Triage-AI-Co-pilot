# Vital Sign Reference Thresholds — ER Triage
Source: ACEP Clinical Policies, AHA Guidelines, Surviving Sepsis Campaign 2021

## Blood Pressure (BP) — mmHg

### Adult Normal Range
Systolic: 90–120 mmHg
Diastolic: 60–80 mmHg

### Thresholds
Hypotension: SBP < 90 mmHg — warrants immediate assessment. ESI upgrade to 2.
Shock: SBP < 90 + altered mental status or signs of poor perfusion = ESI 1.
Prehypertension: SBP 120–139 / DBP 80–89.
Stage 1 Hypertension: SBP 130–139 / DBP 80–89.
Stage 2 Hypertension: SBP ≥ 140 / DBP ≥ 90.
Hypertensive Urgency: SBP ≥ 180 OR DBP ≥ 120 — without end-organ damage.
Hypertensive Emergency: SBP ≥ 180/120 WITH end-organ damage — ESI 2.
Critical upper threshold: SBP ≥ 220 = treat regardless of symptoms.

## Heart Rate (HR) — beats per minute

### Adult Normal Range
60–100 bpm

### Thresholds
Bradycardia: HR < 60 bpm.
Symptomatic Bradycardia: HR < 50 + hypotension, altered mental status, chest pain = ESI 1–2.
Tachycardia: HR > 100 bpm.
Moderate Tachycardia: HR 101–130 = assess for cause (pain, fever, dehydration, anxiety).
Severe Tachycardia: HR > 150 = ESI upgrade to 2. Consider SVT, sepsis, PE, hemorrhage, thyroid storm.
Critical: HR > 180 or < 40 = immediate intervention required. ESI 1.

## Oxygen Saturation (SpO₂) — pulse oximetry

### Normal Range
95–100% on room air.

### Thresholds
Mild hypoxemia: SpO₂ 91–94% — supplemental oxygen, investigate cause. ESI 3 consideration.
Moderate hypoxemia: SpO₂ 88–90% — high-flow oxygen, urgent workup.
Severe hypoxemia: SpO₂ < 88% — immediate intervention. ESI upgrade to 2.
Critical: SpO₂ < 85% = imminent respiratory failure. ESI 1.
Note: COPD patients may have baseline SpO₂ 88–92%; target 88–92% to avoid hypercapnic drive suppression.

## Respiratory Rate (RR) — breaths per minute

### Normal Range
12–20 breaths/min

### Thresholds
Tachypnea: RR > 20 — assess for respiratory distress, sepsis, pain, anxiety, metabolic acidosis.
Significant Tachypnea: RR > 24 — oxygen saturation monitoring, ABG consideration.
Bradypnea: RR < 12 — monitor for respiratory depression (opioid overdose, CNS event).
Critical: RR > 30 or < 8 — ESI upgrade to 2. Immediate airway assessment.

## Temperature — oral/tympanic unless noted

### Normal Range
36.1–37.2°C (97.0–99.0°F)

### Thresholds
Low-grade fever: 37.3–37.9°C.
Fever: ≥ 38.0°C (100.4°F) — warrants source investigation.
High fever: ≥ 39.0°C — sepsis workup if clinical picture supports.
Severe fever: ≥ 39.5°C — high-risk for febrile seizure in pediatrics; adult sepsis concern.
Hyperpyrexia: ≥ 41.0°C — life-threatening. Heatstroke, severe infection, serotonin syndrome. ESI 2.
Hypothermia: < 35.0°C — moderate. < 32°C = severe. Active rewarming. ESI 1–2.

## Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)

### Normal
GCS 15 — fully alert and oriented.

### Thresholds
GCS 13–14: Mild impairment. Monitor closely. ESI 3 minimum.
GCS 9–12: Moderate impairment. Urgent assessment. ESI 2.
GCS ≤ 8: Severe impairment. Airway at risk. Intubation consideration. ESI 1.
GCS 3: No eye, verbal, or motor response. ESI 1.

## Composite Vital Sign Patterns — High Suspicion Triggers

Sepsis triad: Temp > 38.3°C + HR > 90 + RR > 20 = SIRS criteria met. Sepsis workup.
Shock pattern: SBP < 90 + HR > 100 + altered mentation = undifferentiated shock. ESI 1.
PE pattern: HR > 100 + SpO₂ < 94% + RR > 20 + pleuritic chest pain.
Hypertensive emergency pattern: SBP > 180 + new neurological symptoms or chest pain.
Respiratory failure trajectory: SpO₂ < 90% + RR > 30 + accessory muscle use.
